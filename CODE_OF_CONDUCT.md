# Code of Conduct

TextHumanize is built by people with different backgrounds, languages, and
levels of experience. We expect all project spaces to stay respectful,
practical, and focused on improving the software.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive in issues, pull requests, discussions, and
  reviews.
- Assume good intent, but stay precise when reporting bugs or quality gaps.
- Keep feedback technical and actionable.
- Respect privacy. Do not post private, confidential, or copyrighted text
  samples unless you have permission to share them.
- Use inclusive language and avoid harassment, insults, threats, or personal
  attacks.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, doxxing, intimidation, or sexualized language.
- Trolling, repeated off-topic comments, or bad-faith arguments.
- Publishing private security reports before maintainers have had a reasonable
  time to respond.
- Using project channels to request help with deception, fraud, spam, or other
  abusive activity.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove comments, close issues, block participants, or
take other moderation actions when behavior harms the project or contributors.
For serious or repeated violations, maintainers may permanently remove access to
project spaces.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns to the maintainers through GitHub or by email at
ksanyok@me.com. Security vulnerabilities should be reported through the process
in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md), not through public issues.
